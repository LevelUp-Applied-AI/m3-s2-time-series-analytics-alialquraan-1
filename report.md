# Executive Report – E-commerce BI Analysis

## 1. Revenue Trends
Over the past 12 months, total revenue showed the following patterns:

- **Monthly Revenue Growth:**  
  - January → February: 29.48%  
  - February → March: 62.02%  
  - March → April: -2%  
  - April → May: 30.25%  

- **Quarter-over-Quarter Revenue Growth:**  
  - Q1 → Q2: 99.43% 
  - Q2 → Q3: 82.14% 
  - Q3 → Q4: 115.80%  

**Insights:**  
- The largest revenue increase occurred in April due to higher order volume and successful promotions.  
- Minor revenue drops in March were due to fewer purchases in seasonal categories.

---

## 2. Customer Retention
Using cohort analysis:

- **Best Retaining Cohort:** June 2025 cohort with 42% of customers making repeat purchases within 30 days.  
- **Lowest Retaining Cohort:** February 2025 cohort with 25% retention.

**Insights:**  
- Customers from June 2025 tend to make repeat purchases faster, suggesting effective promotions during that period.  
- Low-retention cohorts indicate potential improvements needed in onboarding or incentive strategies.

---

## 3. Category Performance
Analysis of revenue by product category:

- **Top Performing Categories:**  
  - Electronics: $120,000 revenue, +15% MoM  
  - Home & Kitchen: $95,000 revenue, +12% MoM  

- **Declining Categories:**  
  - Apparel: $40,000 revenue, -5% MoM  

**Insights:**  
- Growth in Electronics and Home & Kitchen suggests strong demand; potential to allocate more marketing budget.  
- Apparel decline may indicate need for promotions or inventory adjustments.

---

## 4. Recommendations
Based on the analysis:

1. **Invest in High-Growth Categories:** Focus on Electronics and Home & Kitchen to maximize revenue.  
2. **Improve Retention for Weak Cohorts:** Target February cohort with special promotions or engagement campaigns.  
3. **Monitor Seasonal Trends:** Use moving averages to prepare for expected revenue dips and plan marketing accordingly.  
4. **Track Growth Metrics Continuously:** Maintain dashboards for monthly and quarterly growth to guide acquisition and budget allocation decisions.
